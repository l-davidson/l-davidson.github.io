A Little Bit About Me...

My name is Lydia Davidson and I am currently pursuing a degree in Engineering Physics at the University of British Columbia. I have always been drawn to technology and wanted to develop new things. With a desire to get a deeper understand of how the world around me works, I decided to pursue a degree in Engineering Physics because of its combination of electrical, mechanical, and computer engineering, in addition to providing a solid foundation in physics and mathematics. I believe this degree will provide the foundations that will allow me to achieve my dream of innovating new technology to help others.

Apart from engineering, I enjoy getting active by going for walks with family or going for early morning runs. However, my favourite passtime is probably playing piano. Throughout my 13 years of taking piano lessons, I have learned how perseverance, hard work, and being detail-oriented can produce high-quality results. Playing piano has not only given me fun challenges to tackle when learning and seeking perfection in difficult pieces, but has also been a source of relaxation for me. I love to share my joy of music with others by volunteering at different events, like at a senior's residence in my home town. I have also enjoyed participating in several piano competitions across Quebec and have received the Level 10 Certificate with The Royal Convservatory of Music, which includes exams in practical piano, music history, and harmony. If you are interested, below is a video of me playing at the finals of the Concours de musique de la Capital in Quebec City in 2017, where I tied for first place for my age category.
